# Use your Android Phone as MIDI Input for DAW

1. Connect your Android phone to your PC using a `USB data cable` and change usb connection type to `MIDI`.

   | <img src="usb_notification.jpeg" width=400> | <img src="usb_settings.jpeg" width=400> |
   |:-------------------------------------------:|:---------------------------------------:|

2. Open your DAW software on PC. This guide uses [Reaper](https://www.reaper.fm/).
3. Verify DAW detects your phone as a MIDI Input.

    <img src="midi_input_settings.png" width=400 />

4. Insert a virtual instrument on a new track.

    <img src="insert_virtual_track.png" width=400>

5. Search for **ReaSynth** and load it. ReaSynth is basic synthesizer enough for connection testing purpose. For drums,
   you can try [PowerDrumKit](https://www.powerdrumkit.com/).

   | <img src="search_vst.png" width=400> | <img src="reasynth_vst.png" width=400> |
   |:------------------------------------:|:--------------------------------------:|

6. In your app, edit a pad to add a MIDI note sample and press Play. You should hear sound play from your PC.

   | <img src="edit_pad.png" width=400> | <img src="add_midi.png" width=400> | <img src="edit_midi.png" width=400>  |
   |:----------------------------------:|:----------------------------------:|:------------------------------------:|
